About

An institution for African theatre and cultural memory

AFRIN Arts is a creative organisation committed to producing theatre and cultural experiences rooted in African heritage. Through performance, festivals, and artistic collaboration, we seek to preserve cultural memory while creating opportunities for contemporary creative expression and enterprise. For us, performance is not only an artistic practice but also a means of education, cultural preservation, and community engagement.

AFRIN Arts is founded and led by Jatto Godswill Osose (Jatto Whisper), a Nigerian theatre director, playwright, producer, and cultural strategist whose work explores the intersection of theatre, cultural heritage, indigenous storytelling, and the creative economy. A graduate of the University of Benin, where he earned both a B.A. and an M.A. in Theatre Arts, he combines academic scholarship with practical theatre-making, and serves as Director of Production and Screening for the National Association of Nigerian Theatre Arts Practitioners (NANTAP), Edo State Chapter.

His directing philosophy embraces innovation while respecting tradition. Whether working within conventional theatre spaces or adapting productions for community venues, every production is an opportunity to deepen the relationship between performers, audiences, and the cultural narratives that shape society. Beyond directing, he is invested in institution building: creating platforms that nurture artists, encourage collaboration, and expand opportunities for the performing arts in Nigeria.

The Journey

From a theatre collective to a creative institution

Since beginning his professional theatre practice in 2019, Jatto has built AFRIN Arts through directing, playwriting, production, artist development, and cultural programming, consistently telling African stories in ways that are culturally authentic and relevant to contemporary audiences.

2019

AFRIN Theatre is founded

Jatto directs Oba Ewaure the Great and founds AFRIN Theatre, a creative collective established to produce theatre, develop artists, and promote African storytelling through performance.

2021

AFRIN Theatre becomes AFRIN Arts

The organisation expands beyond theatre into festivals, cultural programming, workshops, and interdisciplinary collaboration. On 14 February 2021, Jatto produces the maiden edition of ESOHE, an original play he wrote, his first major independent work as playwright and producer. He goes on to produce The Chairman, and collaborates with Deepend Entertainment on Sunset at Dawn, written by Andrew Ogbeide.

2023

Enina Theatre Festival

Jatto presents Leke Leke at the Enina Theatre Festival, organised in collaboration with the Edo State Government.

2024

ESOHE wins Best Play

The Enina Theatre Festival staging of ESOHE becomes a defining moment: the production wins Best Play, and Jatto is recognised as both Best Director and Best Playwright. In December, he produces Christmas Homecoming, a seasonal, community-oriented production.

2026

AFRIVORA, first edition

AFRIN Arts launches AFRIVORA, an annual festival of theatre, music, dance, visual arts, cultural heritage, and creative enterprise, opening 10 July 2026 with NEKIGHIDI, a Culture Fest, and a Trade Fair.

Mission & Vision

Why AFRIN Arts exists

Mission

To produce innovative artistic experiences that celebrate African identity, inspire social dialogue, preserve cultural heritage, and empower creative communities through collaboration, education, and performance.

Vision

To become one of Africa's leading creative institutions, recognised for advancing theatre, culture, and the creative economy through impactful productions, festivals, education, and artistic leadership.

Core Values

Artistic Excellence Cultural Authenticity Innovation Collaboration Community Integrity Education Creative Entrepreneurship
